What Is The Diet ?

  • “A particular selection of food, especially as designed or prescribed to improve a person's physical condition or to prevent or treat a disease”
  • Dieting doesn’t always mean losing weight, which is a big misconception — It can mean simply changing the contents/properties of the food you’re eating
  • A diet for someone who wants to lose weight will be different than one for someone who wants to gain muscle for example.

Macro Nutrients

“Macronutrients are the main nutrients that make up the foods we eat. There are three, and you’ve probably heard of them before: Carbohydrates, Protein, and Fat.”

Micro Nutrients

“Micronutrients are small compounds needed in minimum amounts to enable the body to produce enzymes, hormones and other substances essential for healthy growth and development. Commonly referred to as vitamins and minerals, micronutrients include compounds such as Fluoride, Selenium, Sodium, Iodine, Copper, and Zinc. They also include vitamins such as vitamin C, A, D, E, and K, as well as the B-complex vitamins.”

You May Have Different Goals. Here Are Some Tips...

Gaining Muscle

  • Eat many times - about 5 - a day to make it easier to get all of the calories you need.
  • If you don’t want to constantly track your calories, create a meal plan where you have basically the same meals every day.
  • Drink whey protein powder shakes (make sure they have good ingredients). This makes it easier to hit your protein levels.
  • Plan and cook your meals ahead of time so that you don’t have to cook every time you want to eat
  • Eat breakfast

Staying Healthy/Losing Weight

  • Only drink water - This is very simple, but it will cut out a lot of unnatural ingredients in things like soda and alcohol. Also, if you are trying to stay a certain weight or lose weight, this will cut out a lot of calories.
  • No fried food - Fried food has a lot of calories, particularly fat calories.
  • Try to eat complex carbohydrates - These are foods like oatmeal, rice, whole grain bread, potatoes, quinoa, and beans. They digest slower than simple carbs, which makes it easier to stay full
  • Have cheat days - It’s great to eat completely healthy all year, but realistically, this is extremely hard for most people. Having a cheat day once a week will keep you motivated because it acts as a reward for eating well. A cheat day doesn’t mean you should eat the most unhealthy foods ever made. Still, use common sense.
