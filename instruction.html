Workout Structure

By Joseph Gandolfo

     Workout structure is important for anyone who is starting to exercise. After an intense workout, you will be sore and your body will be tired from the physical activity and the stress you put it under. Properly spacing your routines will make sure you have time to recover from each session and will help drastically reduce your chances of getting injured in the process. Workout structure helps you make sure you don’t undertrain or overtrain. If you hit all your muscle groups in one day, and are sore and out of the gym for a week, you won’t be able to perform as well and it will take a long time to hit your goals. If you train too much, you will not give your body enough time to rebuild itself and it can negatively affect your muscles and your hormones too.

     A great workout structure for beginners should include days where many parts of your body are included and aren’t that specific. You should also give yourself a good amount of rest throughout the week because your body will be sore due to the fact it is not used to the activities yet. It is important to stretch or foam roll parts of your body that are sore or tight after you workout to make sure it doesn’t affect you. Being tight and inflexible can put more stress on other parts of your body and may cause you to have bad form during your workouts. An example of a workout structure and one that will work well for beginners is:

  • Monday: Upper Body
  • Tuesday: Lower Body
  • Wednesday: Rest
  • Thursday: Upper Body
  • Friday: Lower Body
  • Saturday: Core
  • Sunday: Rest

     Along with workout structure comes rep ranges, which is an individual structure to each exercise you are doing. Different rep ranges will have different effects on your muscles and your body. The main types of rep ranges are 3-5, 8-12, and 15+.

  • 3-5 Rep Range: This range is for your heaviest or hardest exercises. You should perform these safely, with good form, and a spotter if needed because it usually requires close to maximum effort from your body. This rep range will not help you gain muscle size or lose fat, but instead helps to increase your strength. It conditions your nervous system and tendons to be able to handle heavier loads. With training in this range, you will see your numbers go up in weight lifted and feel other exercises get a little easier. Because of the difficulty of the exercise, it also serves as a great way to burn fat. Your body continues to burn calories throughout the day and it raises your metabolism.

  • 8-12 Rep Range: This range is for building up your muscles and making them grow. The 8-12 range involves using a weight that is heavy for you but just enough in order to get a medium number of reps. The last reps in this range should be very difficult and should put the most stress on your muscles. These are the most important reps because its the time when the fibers in your muscle are most likely to break and tear so that they can go through the process of hypertrophy. Hypertrophy is the enlargement of tissues due to the increase in size of its cells.This is what causes your muscle to grow bigger and stronger when it recovers from the workout.

  • 15+ Rep Range: This range is used for building up your muscle endurance and muscle tone. In this range you should be using little weight and many times be doing the exercise until failure, or until you can’t do any more reps. This helps you with your endurance because it trains your body and target muscle to deal with lactic acid that accumulates in the area throughout your sets. The better your body is at clearing out this acid, the longer it will be able to perform in the future. Because this rep range also provides a long time under tension, there is a lot of blood flow that is recruited during the set. This, along with the high count of stretches and contractions your muscle goes through, helps to form a toned muscle. A toned muscle does not necessarily mean there is less body fat in that area, but means that it is able to sit in a more contracted state when you are not doing anything. This gives the appearance that there is less fat because it is popping out more.
