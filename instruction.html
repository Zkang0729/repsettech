Workout Structure

     Workout structure is important for anyone who is starting to exercise. After an intense workout, you will be sore and your body will be tired from the physical activity and the stress you put it under. Properly spacing your routines will make sure you have time to recover from each session and will help drastically reduce your chances of getting injured in the process. Workout structure helps you make sure you don’t undertrain or overtrain. If you hit all your muscle groups in one day, and are sore and out of the gym for a week, you won’t be able to perform as well and it will take a long time to hit your goals. If you train too much, you will not give your body enough time to rebuild itself and it can negatively affect your muscles and your hormones too.

     A great workout structure for beginners should include days where many parts of your body are included and aren’t that specific. You should also give yourself a good amount of rest throughout the week because your body will be sore due to the fact it is not used to the activities yet. It is important to stretch or foam roll parts of your body that are sore or tight after you workout to make sure it doesn’t affect you. Being tight and inflexible can put more stress on other parts of your body and may cause you to have bad form during your workouts. An example of a workout structure and one that will work well for beginners is:

  • Monday: Upper Body
  • Tuesday: Lower Body
  • Wednesday: Rest
  • Thursday: Upper Body
  • Friday: Lower Body
  • Saturday: Core
  • Sunday: Rest
